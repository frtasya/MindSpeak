Mental Health Issues

Depression

Mood disorder characterised by lowering of mood, loss of interest and enjoyment, and reduced energy. Depression can lead to increased risk of suicidal thoughts or behaviour.

Anxiety Disorders

People are troubled by excessive worrying that seems difficult to control.

Post-traumatic Stress Disorder

PTSD is a mental health condition that can develop as a response to people who have experienced any traumatic event.

Bipolar Affective Disorder

Mental health condition that has cycles of extreme low and high mood.

Dissociative Disorder

Mental process where a person disconnects from their thoughts, feelings, memories or sense of identity.

Eating Disorder

Body dissatisfaction and dieting are two risk factors for the onset of eating disorders.

Obsessive Compulsive Disorder

Obsessions are recurrent thoughts, images, or impulses that are intrusive and unwanted.

Psychotic Disorder

People affected by psychosis can experience delusions, hallucinations and confused thinking.

Schizophrenia

Complex psychotic disorder characterised by disruptions to thinking and emotions, and a distorted perception of reality.
